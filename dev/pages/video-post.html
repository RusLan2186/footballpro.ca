Arsenal pulled off a sensational comeback in the Premier League as they defeated Manchester United 3-2 in a thrilling encounter at the Emirates Stadium. The game appeared to be slipping away from Arsenal when United took a 2-1 lead with just 10 minutes remaining. However, the Gunners showed resilience and determination, scoring two quick goals in the dying minutes to snatch all three points.

Manchester United opened the scoring in the first half with a well-taken goal by their striker, James Thompson, who found the back of the net after a precise cross from Marcus Rashford. Arsenal responded shortly after, leveling the score when Gabriel Martinelli capitalized on a defensive mistake to slot the ball past United’s goalkeeper, David De Gea.

The second half saw both teams battle fiercely for the lead, with Arsenal having a majority of possession but struggling to break down United's defense. As the match entered its final stages, Manchester United regained the lead after Bruno Fernandes' clever free-kick was flicked on by Harry Maguire, beating Arsenal’s goalkeeper Aaron Ramsdale.

With time running out, Arsenal’s manager made key substitutions that turned the game in their favor. In the 82nd minute, Martin Ødegaard delivered a brilliant ball into the box, which was met by striker Eddie Nketiah, who powered it into the net to equalize. The momentum shifted, and just two minutes later, Arsenal’s captain, Martin Ødegaard, scored the winner with a stunning long-range strike that found the top corner of the net, leaving De Gea no chance.
